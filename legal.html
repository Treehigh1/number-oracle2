Responsible Gambling

Number Oracle is committed to promoting responsible gambling awareness. While we are an entertainment and analysis tool — not a gambling service — we recognise that our users may play lottery games.

Important Reminders

  • Lottery is entertainment, not income. Never spend more than you can comfortably afford to lose.
  • Every draw is random. Past results, "hot" numbers, and statistical patterns do not influence future outcomes. Each draw is independent.
  • Set a budget. Decide how much you're willing to spend on lottery tickets per week or month, and stick to it.
  • Don't chase losses. If you haven't won, buying more tickets doesn't improve your odds in any meaningful way.
  • It's OK to take a break. If lottery play is causing you stress or financial difficulty, step away.

Warning Signs

You may need help if you:

  • Spend more on lottery tickets than you can afford
  • Borrow money or sell things to buy lottery tickets
  • Feel anxious, stressed, or depressed about gambling
  • Lie to family or friends about how much you spend
  • Neglect work, family, or other responsibilities
